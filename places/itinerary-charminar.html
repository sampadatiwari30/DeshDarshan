2 Days in Hyderabad: Charminar & Old City

This 2-day itinerary explores the iconic Charminar, the heart of Hyderabad's Old City. Experience the rich Qutb Shahi heritage, bustling markets, and the famous Hyderabadi cuisine that makes this area so special.

Trip Highlights

  • Charminar monument visit
  • Hyderabadi biryani tasting
  • Laad Bazaar shopping
  • Mecca Masjid visit
  • Pearl market exploration

Unique Facts About Charminar

Symbol of Hyderabad

Built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a deadly plague and to mark the city's founding.

400+ Years Old

This iconic monument has stood at the heart of Hyderabad for over four centuries, witnessing the city's growth.

Four Grand Arches

The structure's four grand arches face the cardinal directions and were meant to be the center of the planned city.

Mosque at the Top

The western end of the roof has a mosque with 45 prayer spaces, making it one of the oldest surviving mosques in Hyderabad.

Diamond Trade Center

Charminar was at the center of Hyderabad's global diamond and pearl trade during the Qutb Shahi period.

Illumination

The monument is beautifully illuminated every evening, creating a spectacular sight against the night sky.

Day 1: Charminar & Old City

9:00 AM

Charminar Visit

Explore this iconic 16th-century monument built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. Climb to the top for panoramic views of the bustling old city.

11:00 AM

Mecca Masjid

Visit one of India's largest mosques built with bricks made from soil brought from Mecca. Marvel at its grand architecture and peaceful courtyard.

1:30 PM

Lunch at Shah Ghouse

Savor authentic Hyderabadi biryani at this legendary restaurant near Charminar, known for its flavorful spices and tender meat.

3:30 PM

Laad Bazaar

Stroll through this vibrant market known for lacquer bangles, pearls, traditional attire, and other Hyderabadi specialties.

7:00 PM

Evening at Hussain Sagar

Visit the heart-shaped lake with its giant Buddha statue and enjoy the musical fountain show in the evening.

Day 2: Golconda & Pearls

8:00 AM

Golconda Fort

Explore this magnificent fort known for its acoustic architecture and diamond mining history. Don't miss the light and sound show in the evening.

12:00 PM

Qutb Shahi Tombs

Visit the majestic tombs of the Qutb Shahi rulers, showcasing a unique blend of Persian and Indian architecture.

2:00 PM

Lunch at Paradise

Enjoy another round of Hyderabadi delicacies at this iconic restaurant known for its biryani and kebabs.

4:00 PM

Pearl Market

Explore Hyderabad's famous pearl markets where you can find everything from affordable freshwater pearls to exquisite jewelry.

7:00 PM

Night at Charminar

Return to see Charminar beautifully illuminated at night and enjoy the vibrant street food scene in the area.

Travel Tips for Hyderabad

Best Time to Visit

October to February for pleasant weather. Avoid summer months (April-June) when temperatures can soar above 40°C.

Dress Code

Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Comfortable footwear is essential for exploring the old city's uneven streets.

Language

Telugu and Urdu are widely spoken. English and Hindi are understood in tourist areas.

Shopping Tips

Bargain at Laad Bazaar. For pearls, visit government-certified shops for authenticity certificates.

Essential Information

Opening Hours

  • Charminar: 9:30 AM - 5:30 PM daily
  • Mecca Masjid: 4:00 AM - 9:30 PM (closed to non-Muslims during prayers)
  • Golconda Fort: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

Entry Fees

  • Charminar: ₹25 (Indians), ₹300 (Foreigners)
  • Golconda Fort: ₹25 (Indians), ₹300 (Foreigners)
  • Qutb Shahi Tombs: ₹20 (Indians), ₹100 (Foreigners)

Getting There

  • By Air: Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (22km from city center)
  • By Train: Hyderabad Deccan Station (Nampally) is well connected
  • By Metro: Charminar metro station is just 1km from the monument
