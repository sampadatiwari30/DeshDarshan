2 Days in Mumbai: Gateway of India & Colonial Heritage

This 2-day itinerary explores Mumbai's iconic Gateway of India and its surrounding colonial-era landmarks. Experience the blend of British architecture, Indian culture, and coastal beauty that defines this vibrant city.

Trip Highlights

  • Gateway of India at sunset
  • Elephanta Caves boat trip
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus
  • Colaba seafood experience
  • Taj Mahal Palace Hotel

Day 1: Gateway & Colaba

4:00 PM

Gateway of India

Start your exploration of this 26-meter tall basalt arch built during British rule. Enjoy the sea breeze and watch street performers.

6:00 PM

Taj Mahal Palace Hotel

Visit this iconic hotel adjacent to the Gateway. Enjoy high tea at the Sea Lounge with views of the harbor.

8:00 PM

Dinner at Trishna

Savor Mumbai's famous butter garlic crab and other seafood specialties at this legendary Colaba restaurant.

Day 2: Elephanta & Colonial Architecture

9:00 AM

Elephanta Caves

Take the ferry from Gateway to this UNESCO site with ancient rock-cut cave temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.

2:00 PM

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

Visit this UNESCO-listed Victorian Gothic railway station, a masterpiece of British architecture.

4:00 PM

Marine Drive Sunset

End your trip with a stroll along this iconic curved promenade as the sun sets over the Arabian Sea.

Travel Tips for Gateway of India

Best Time to Visit: November to February for pleasant weather. Evenings are most vibrant.

Elephanta Caves: Ferries run from 9AM-2PM. Check tide conditions before planning your trip.

Photography: The Gateway looks spectacular at golden hour. Get creative with framing using the arch.

Dress Code: Comfortable clothes and shoes for walking. Modest dress for Elephanta Caves.
