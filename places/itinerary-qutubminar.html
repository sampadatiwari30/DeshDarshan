2 Days in Delhi: Qutub Minar & Historical Exploration

This 2-day itinerary takes you through Delhi's rich history centered around the Qutub Minar complex, the tallest brick minaret in the world. Explore the architectural marvels of the Delhi Sultanate period and other nearby historical gems. The Qutub Minar complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the beginning of Muslim rule in India.

Unique Facts About Qutub Minar

Tallest Brick Minaret

At 73 meters (239.5 feet), Qutub Minar is the world's tallest brick minaret. It has 379 steps leading to the top, though public access to the upper levels has been restricted since 1981.

Centuries in the Making

Construction began in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, but the tower was completed in 1368 by Firoz Shah Tughlaq. The different architectural styles reflect the different periods of construction.

Mysterious Iron Pillar

The 7-meter tall Iron Pillar in the complex has stood rust-free for over 1600 years, showcasing ancient India's advanced metallurgical skills. Its inscriptions date back to 4th century CE.

Five Distinct Stories

The minaret has five distinct stories, each marked by a projecting balcony. The first three are made of red sandstone, the fourth and fifth of marble and sandstone.

First Mosque in India

The Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque in the complex was the first mosque built in India after the Islamic conquest. It was constructed using materials from 27 demolished Hindu and Jain temples.

Lightning Strikes

The minaret was struck by lightning in 1368, damaging the top story. Firoz Shah Tughlaq rebuilt it and added the current two stories, replacing the original cupola with a dome.

Day 1: Qutub Complex & Mehrauli

9:00 AM

Qutub Minar Complex

Begin your day at the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Qutub Minar. Explore the 73-meter tall minaret, Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, Iron Pillar, and Alai Darwaza. Marvel at the intricate carvings and Indo-Islamic architecture. Don't miss the beautiful calligraphy inscriptions on the minaret.

12:00 PM

Lunch at Olive Qutub

Enjoy Mediterranean cuisine in the beautiful surroundings of Olive restaurant, set in a restored 19th-century building near the Qutub complex. Try their signature dishes like burrata salad and wood-fired pizzas in the lush garden setting.

2:00 PM

Mehrauli Archaeological Park

Explore this 200-acre park containing over 100 historically significant monuments, including Balban's Tomb and Jamali Kamali Mosque. The park spans over 1000 years of Delhi's history, from the 10th century to the British era.

Day 2: Delhi's Heritage Trail

9:00 AM

Hauz Khas Village

Visit this medieval village with its historic water tank, madrasa, and tomb of Feroz Shah Tughlaq. Enjoy the blend of history and contemporary art galleries. The deer park offers a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle.

12:00 PM

Dilli Haat

Experience crafts and cuisines from all over India at this open-air market. Perfect for souvenir shopping and trying regional foods from different states. Don't miss the handloom textiles and traditional jewelry.

3:00 PM

Lodhi Gardens

End your trip with a stroll through these beautiful gardens dotted with 15th-century tombs of the Sayyid and Lodhi dynasties. The gardens are particularly beautiful in the late afternoon light, perfect for photography.

Travel Tips for Qutub Minar

Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather. Visit early morning (8-10am) to avoid crowds and harsh sunlight. The complex is open from sunrise to sunset.

Tickets: ₹40 for Indians, ₹600 for foreigners. Combined ticket available for Qutub Minar and other monuments in the complex. Free entry for children below 15 years.

Photography: The Iron Pillar and intricate carvings on the minaret make excellent photo subjects. Best light for photography is early morning or late afternoon.

Dress Code: Comfortable walking shoes as the complex has uneven surfaces. Modest clothing recommended as it's a historical religious site.

Guides: Licensed guides available at entrance. Audio guides also available in multiple languages. Download the ASI's Qutub Minar app for self-guided tours.
