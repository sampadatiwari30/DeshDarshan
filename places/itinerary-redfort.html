2 Days in Delhi: Red Fort & Mughal Heritage

This 2-day itinerary explores the majestic Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the political hub of Mughal India. Discover the grandeur of Mughal architecture and the vibrant culture of Old Delhi. The Red Fort, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years and remains a powerful symbol of India's rich history.

Unique Facts About Red Fort

Independence Day Tradition

Every year on August 15th, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the Red Fort's Lahori Gate and delivers a speech to the nation, continuing a tradition started in 1947.

Lost Treasures

The fort originally housed the legendary Peacock Throne and Kohinoor diamond, both looted by Persian invader Nadir Shah in 1739. The throne alone was worth twice the cost of the Taj Mahal.

Architectural Fusion

The Red Fort blends Persian, Timurid, and Hindu architectural styles. Its name comes from the massive red sandstone walls that stretch over 2 kilometers and stand 33 meters high.

Advanced Water System

The fort featured an ingenious water cooling system where water from the Yamuna river flowed through channels in the marble floors to cool the palace rooms during Delhi's scorching summers.

Original Name

Shah Jahan named it "Qila-e-Mubarak" (The Blessed Fort). The British renamed it "Red Fort" after the red sandstone used in its construction.

Construction Time

Built in just 9 years (1639-1648), the fort required over 10,000 workers daily. Its construction cost was about 10 million rupees (approx ₹70 billion today).

Day 1: Red Fort & Old Delhi

9:00 AM

Red Fort Exploration

Begin at the Lahore Gate, walk through Chatta Chowk bazaar, and explore the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), Rang Mahal, and other structures within this massive fort complex. Don't miss the intricate pietra dura inlay work.

12:00 PM

Lunch at Karim's

Enjoy legendary Mughlai cuisine at this century-old restaurant near Jama Masjid, famous for its mutton burra, kebabs, and biryanis. Established in 1913, it's been serving royal recipes passed down from Mughal chefs.

2:00 PM

Jama Masjid

Visit India's largest mosque, built by Shah Jahan between 1644-1656. Climb the 121-step southern minaret for panoramic views of Old Delhi. The mosque can accommodate 25,000 worshippers in its courtyard.

4:00 PM

Chandni Chowk Walk

Explore the bustling lanes of Old Delhi's main market, visiting the spice markets at Khari Baoli, the cloth shops at Katra Neel, and historic havelis. Try local street food like parathas at Paranthe Wali Gali and jalebis at Old Famous Jalebi Wala.

Day 2: Museums & Light Show

10:00 AM

Red Fort Museums

Visit the archaeological museum (with artifacts from Mughal era) and war memorial museum within the fort complex to learn about Delhi's history. The museum in Naubat Khana displays weapons, uniforms, and paintings from the 1857 uprising.

1:00 PM

Lunch at Haldiram's

Experience authentic North Indian vegetarian cuisine at this iconic restaurant. Try their famous chole bhature, raj kachori, and sweets like rasmalai. The outlet near Chandni Chowk is one of their original locations.

3:00 PM

National Museum

Explore artifacts from India's 5000-year history at this premier museum near India Gate. Highlights include relics from the Harappan civilization, Buddhist art, and Mughal miniature paintings.

7:00 PM

Red Fort Light Show

End your trip with the spectacular sound and light show that narrates Delhi's history through the voices of its legendary rulers (check timings as per season). The show brings alive the fort's history in Hindi and English on alternate days.

Travel Tips for Red Fort

Best Time to Visit: October to March for pleasant weather. Summer months (April-June) can be extremely hot. The light show timings vary by season (usually 7:30-8:30pm in summer, 6-7pm in winter).

Tickets: ₹35 for Indians, ₹500 for foreigners. Separate tickets for fort entry and museums. Friday closed. Photography allowed (no tripod without permission).

Dress Comfortably: Wear comfortable shoes as there's lots of walking (the complex spans 254 acres). Modest clothing recommended for visiting Jama Masjid (robes available for rent).

Photography: The fort looks spectacular in morning light. Best photography spots include the Diwan-i-Khas, Lahori Gate, and the water channels in Hayat Bakhsh Bagh.

Security: Only small bags allowed (lockers available). Arrive early to avoid long queues at security check. Metro access via Chandni Chowk station (Yellow Line).
