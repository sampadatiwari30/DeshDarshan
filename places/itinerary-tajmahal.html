3 Days in Agra: The Ultimate Taj Mahal Experience

This 3-day itinerary takes you through the wonders of Agra, home to the magnificent Taj Mahal. Experience the best of Mughal architecture, local cuisine, and cultural heritage with this carefully planned guide. From the iconic ivory-white mausoleum to the bustling local markets, this journey will immerse you in the rich history and vibrant culture of this UNESCO World Heritage city.

Trip Highlights

  • Sunset & sunrise views of Taj Mahal
  • Authentic Mughlai cuisine
  • Agra Fort exploration
  • Local market shopping
  • Mehtab Bagh moonlight views

Unique Facts About Taj Mahal

Symbol of Love

Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth.

22 Years to Build

Construction took 22 years (1631-1653) and employed 20,000 workers and 1,000 elephants.

Changing Colors

The marble appears pinkish in morning, milky white in evening, and golden when lit by moonlight.

Precious Materials

Decorated with 28 types of precious and semi-precious stones inlaid in white marble.

Perfect Symmetry

The entire complex is perfectly symmetrical except for Shah Jahan's cenotaph which was added later.

Construction Cost

Estimated cost was about 32 million rupees (approx $1 billion in today's value).

Day 1: Arrival & Taj Mahal at Sunset

2:00 PM

Arrival in Agra

Check into your hotel and freshen up. We recommend staying at a heritage property like The Oberoi Amarvilas for stunning Taj views or ITC Mughal for a luxurious experience with Mughal-inspired architecture.

4:00 PM

Visit Mehtab Bagh

Start with the Moonlight Garden across the Yamuna River for a breathtaking view of the Taj Mahal from a different perspective. This charbagh complex was designed as the final Mughal garden with perfect alignment to view the Taj.

6:00 PM

Taj Mahal at Sunset

Enter the Taj Mahal complex in the evening when the crowds thin out and the marble glows in the golden light. Marvel at the intricate pietra dura inlay work and hear the romantic story behind this monument to love.

Day 2: Agra Fort & Local Experiences

5:30 AM

Taj Mahal at Sunrise

Experience the magical sight of the Taj Mahal at dawn when it's bathed in soft morning light. Arrive early to witness the changing colors of the marble as the sun rises. Don't forget your camera for these iconic shots!

9:00 AM

Breakfast & Relax

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel or try local specialties at Joney's Place, famous for its stuffed parathas and lassi. Sample Agra's famous petha (sweet candy) from Panchhi Petha shop.

11:00 AM

Agra Fort Exploration

Visit the massive red sandstone fort that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. See the beautiful Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), and the Musamman Burj where Shah Jahan was imprisoned.

2:00 PM

Lunch & Shopping

Enjoy authentic Mughlai cuisine at Pinch of Spice or Peshawri. Then explore Kinari Bazaar for marble handicrafts, leather goods, and jewelry - perfect souvenirs to take home.

Day 3: Fatehpur Sikri & Departure

8:00 AM

Fatehpur Sikri Excursion

Take a day trip to this abandoned Mughal city, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the stunning Buland Darwaza (Gate of Magnificence), Jama Masjid, and the tomb of Sufi saint Salim Chishti.

1:00 PM

Final Taj Mahal Visit

Return to Agra for one last look at the Taj Mahal from different angles. Visit the Taj Museum to learn more about its construction and history before saying goodbye.

4:00 PM

Departure

Check out from your hotel with unforgettable memories of this architectural wonder. Consider stopping at Sikandra to see Akbar's tomb on your way out of Agra.

Travel Tips for Agra

Best Time to Visit: The ideal months are October to March when the weather is cooler. The Taj is closed on Fridays.

Local Cuisine: Don't miss trying Agra's famous petha (sweet), bedai with sabzi (spicy breakfast), and Mughlai cuisine at Pinch of Spice or Peshawri restaurants.

Tickets & Guides: Purchase combo tickets for multiple monuments. Hire only ASI-approved guides at the monuments for authentic information.

Photography: The Taj looks stunning at sunrise. Tripods aren't allowed inside. The "Diana Bench" spot offers classic photo opportunities.

Shopping: Look for marble inlay work, leather goods, and jewelry. Bargain politely in markets. Visit government emporiums for authentic crafts.
