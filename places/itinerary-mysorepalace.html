2 Days in Mysore: Palace & Royal Heritage

This 2-day itinerary explores the magnificent Mysore Palace, one of India's most spectacular royal residences. Experience the grandeur of the Wodeyar dynasty, Mysore's rich culture, and its famous silk and sandalwood. The palace, illuminated by nearly 100,000 bulbs on Sundays and during festivals, is a sight to behold against the night sky.

Trip Highlights

  • Mysore Palace illumination
  • Chamundi Hill visit
  • Devaraja Market shopping
  • Mysore pak tasting
  • Sandalwood products
  • Brindavan Gardens

Unique Facts About Mysore Palace

Royal Residence

The official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty, rulers of Mysore for over 600 years, though the current structure was rebuilt after a fire in 1897.

100,000 Lights

During special occasions, the palace is illuminated with nearly 100,000 light bulbs, creating a breathtaking spectacle.

Architectural Blend

Showcases a unique blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic architectural styles (Indo-Saracenic style).

Golden Howdah

Houses the famous golden howdah (elephant throne) weighing 750kg of gold, used during Dasara processions.

Intricate Artwork

Features exquisite ceilings with peacock motifs, stained glass windows, and intricate wood carvings throughout.

Dasara Celebrations

The palace is the main venue for Mysore Dasara, a 10-day festival that attracts visitors from across the world.

Day 1: Palace & City Tour

10:00 AM

Mysore Palace Tour

Explore this Indo-Saracenic marvel with its ornate durbar hall, carved doors, and the famous golden howdah. See the palace's exquisite paintings and royal artifacts. Don't miss the intricate peacock motifs in the Kalyana Mantapa (marriage hall).

1:00 PM

Lunch at Hotel RRR

Savor authentic Karnataka thali meals at this iconic local restaurant. Try their signature dishes like bisi bele bath, ragi mudde, and Mysore masala dosa.

3:00 PM

Devaraja Market

Explore this vibrant market for Mysore silk, sandalwood products, spices, and the famous Mysore pak sweet. The flower section is particularly photogenic with its colorful displays.

7:00 PM

Palace Illumination

Return to see the palace lit up with nearly 100,000 bulbs (Sunday evenings and during Dasara). The golden glow against the night sky creates a magical atmosphere.

Day 2: Chamundi Hill & Surroundings

8:00 AM

Chamundi Hill

Climb the 1000 steps or drive up to visit the Chamundeshwari Temple and enjoy panoramic views of Mysore. See the giant Nandi statue and the Mahishasura monument along the way.

11:00 AM

Brindavan Gardens

Visit these beautiful terraced gardens with musical fountain shows (evenings are best). The gardens are built across the Kaveri River and feature symmetrical designs, boat rides, and light displays.

2:00 PM

Lunch at The Old House

Enjoy continental and local cuisine in this heritage bungalow setting. Their filter coffee and Mysore-style biryani come highly recommended.

4:00 PM

Rail Museum

Explore vintage locomotives and the royal train coaches used by the Wodeyars. The museum features India's first steam engine and a charming toy train ride.

Travel Tips for Mysore

Best Time to Visit

October to March for pleasant weather. September-October for Dasara festival when the palace is spectacularly decorated with lights and cultural programs.

Palace Etiquette

Remove footwear before entering palace areas. Photography prohibited inside main palace building. Dress modestly as it's a heritage and religious site.

Getting Around

Auto-rickshaws are convenient for short distances. Consider hiring a car for full-day sightseeing. Ola/Uber available. Palace area is walkable.

Shopping Tips

Buy authentic Mysore silk at KSIC showrooms. Bargain at Devaraja Market but avoid overly cheap sandalwood which may be fake. Look for GI-tagged Mysore paintings.

Food Recommendations

Must-try: Mysore pak, masala dosa, filter coffee. Popular eateries: Mylari for dosas, Guru Sweet Mart for sweets, Vinayaka Mylari for meals.

Money Matters

Carry cash for small vendors and auto-rickshaws. Most shops and restaurants accept cards. ATMs widely available around palace area.

Essential Information

Opening Hours

  • Mysore Palace: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM (last entry 5:15 PM)
  • Illumination: 7:00 PM - 7:45 PM (Sundays & holidays)
  • Chamundi Temple: 7:30 AM - 2:00 PM, 3:30 PM - 6:00 PM
  • Brindavan Gardens: 6:00 AM - 9:00 AM, 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM
  • Rail Museum: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM (Closed Mondays)

Entry Fees

  • Mysore Palace: ₹70 (Indians), ₹200 (Foreigners), ₹25 (Students)
  • Brindavan Gardens: ₹15 (Indians), ₹50 (Foreigners)
  • Rail Museum: ₹20 (Adults), ₹10 (Children)
  • Chamundi Hill: No entry fee (Parking ₹30)
  • Camera Fees: ₹50-200 at various sites

Getting There

  • By Air: Nearest airport is Bengaluru (170km, 3.5hr drive)
  • By Train: Well connected to Bengaluru (2.5hrs), Chennai, Mumbai
  • By Road: 3hr drive from Bengaluru via NH275 (Excellent highway)
  • Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws charge ₹15-20/km. Taxis available for ₹1500-2000/day
