Qutub Minar

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi

About Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, India, is the tallest brick minaret in the world and one of the most famous historical landmarks in India. Built in the early 13th century, it stands as a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Key Highlights

  • Height: 73 meters (240 feet)
  • Construction began: 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak
  • Made of red sandstone and marble
  • Contains intricate carvings and verses from the Quran
  • Surrounded by other monuments such as the Iron Pillar and Alai Darwaza

Interesting Facts

  1. The Qutub Minar has 379 steps leading to the top.
  2. It tilts slightly, but remains stable even after earthquakes.
  3. The Iron Pillar nearby has not rusted for over 1600 years.
  4. It symbolizes the beginning of Muslim rule in India.
