Subhash Chandra Bose

Leader of the Azad Hind movement and the Indian National Army

Biography INA & Azad Hind Ideology & Slogans Timeline Legacy

Subhash Chandra Bose (1897–presumed 1945) was a prominent nationalist who championed uncompromising resistance against British rule. Charismatic, disciplined, and strategically bold, he mobilized support in India and abroad to seek independence through the Provisional Government of Azad Hind and the Indian National Army (INA).

  • Twice elected President of the Indian National Congress (1938, 1939).
  • Advocated complete independence and organized mass mobilization beyond constitutional methods.
  • Formed the INA and sought international alliances to liberate India.

INA & Provisional Government of Azad Hind

  • Revitalized the INA in Southeast Asia and provided a clear military objective.
  • Established the Provisional Government of Azad Hind to assert sovereignty and legitimacy.
  • Promoted inclusive ranks, including the Rani Jhansi Regiment of women soldiers.
  • Advanced the call of self-sacrifice, discipline, and unity for national freedom.

Ideology & Slogans

  • Demanded complete independence with a strong, modern, and social-justice–oriented India.
  • Emphasized national planning, industrial growth, and scientific progress.
  • Promoted unity beyond region, religion, language, and caste.
  • Associated rallying calls included: “Give me blood; I will give you freedom” and “Dilli Chalo”.

Key Timeline

1897 Birth in Cuttack, Odisha

Born on 23 January 1897; educated at Presidency College and University of Calcutta.

1920s–30s Rise in national politics

Served in Congress leadership; advocated complete independence and rapid industrialization.

1938–39 Congress President

Elected Congress President; resigned after differences on strategy and wartime policy.

1943 Azad Hind Government

Assumed leadership of the INA and formed the Provisional Government of Azad Hind in Singapore.

1944 INA campaigns

INA advanced toward India’s eastern front; symbolically hoisted the tricolour in Indian territory.

1945 Final phase

Last known movements during the closing stages of World War II; legacy grew in post-war India.

Legacy & Memorials

National Impact

Inspired armed resistance narratives and widened the scope of the freedom struggle.

Institutions

Commemorated through research centers, museums, and civic institutions across India.

Remembrance

23 January is observed to honor his contribution and leadership.
