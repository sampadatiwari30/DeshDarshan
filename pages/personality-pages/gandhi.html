Mahatma Gandhi

Father of the Nation & Apostle of Non-Violence

Overview

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (1869–1948), known as Mahatma Gandhi, was the leader of India's non-violent independence movement against British colonial rule. He inspired millions through his principles of Ahimsa (non-violence) and Satyagraha (truth and civil resistance).

Gandhi's simple lifestyle, moral courage, and relentless fight for justice made him a global symbol of peace and human rights.

Did You Know? 🤔

🕊 Gandhi walked over 240 miles during the Dandi Salt March in 1930.
📜 He studied law in London before starting his activism in South Africa.
👓 His round glasses became an enduring symbol of his identity.
