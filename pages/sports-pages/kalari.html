🥋 Kalaripayattu – The Ancient Martial Art

📖 Origin & History

Kalaripayattu is one of the oldest martial arts in the world, originating in Kerala, India, around 3,000 years ago. It was practiced to train warriors in agility, strength, and discipline. The word comes from “Kalari” (battlefield/gymnasium) and “Payattu” (fight or practice). Known for its graceful movements, weapons training, and healing techniques, it is often called the “Mother of Martial Arts.”

💡 Did You Know?

🌍 Kalaripayattu influenced martial arts in China and Japan.
⚔ Practitioners train with swords, spears, and even bare hands.
💆 It includes healing practices like massage & Ayurveda.
🎭 Its movements inspired classical dances like Kathakali.

🌟 Modern Kalaripayattu

Today, Kalaripayattu is taught in traditional schools called Kalaris across Kerala. It has gained international recognition and is performed in cultural festivals worldwide. Beyond self-defense, it is celebrated as a blend of martial art, dance, yoga, and Ayurveda. Even Bollywood and Hollywood films have drawn inspiration from its moves.
