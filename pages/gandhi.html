Mahatma Gandhi

"Father of the Nation" – Advocate of Non-Violence and Truth

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (1869–1948), fondly called "Bapu", was the leader of India’s independence movement and a global symbol of peace, truth, and non-violence.

Key Contributions

  • ✨ Led India’s freedom struggle through the philosophy of Ahimsa (Non-violence) and Satyagraha (Truth-force).
  • ✨ Championed social reforms like removal of untouchability and upliftment of rural communities.
  • ✨ Led iconic movements such as Champaran Satyagraha, Salt March, and Quit India Movement.
  • ✨ Inspired leaders and movements for civil rights and freedom across the world (e.g., Martin Luther King Jr., Nelson Mandela).

Famous Quotes

“Be the change that you wish to see in the world.”
“In a gentle way, you can shake the world.”
“The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.”

Legacy

Gandhi’s teachings of peace and truth continue to inspire movements worldwide. His birthday, 2nd October, is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti in India and as the International Day of Non-Violence globally.
