Rani Laxmi Bai

Queen of Jhansi | Warrior of Indian Rebellion 1857

Welcome to the Legacy of Rani Laxmi Bai

The fearless queen who led Jhansi’s fight against British rule.

Key Facts

  • Full Name: Manikarnika Rao or Laxmi Bai
  • Born: 19 November 1828, Varanasi, India
  • Known as: Rani of Jhansi
  • Philosophy: Courage, Valor, Leadership
  • Occupation: Queen, Military Leader

Historical Significance

  • Leader in the 1857 Indian Rebellion against British East India Company.
  • Fought bravely to protect Jhansi from British annexation.
  • Inspired resistance and unity among Indian soldiers and civilians.
  • Symbol of female empowerment and courage in Indian history.

Philosophy

  • Believed in defending one’s kingdom and people at all costs.
  • Promoted bravery and fearlessness among her troops.
  • Refused to accept injustice or oppression.
  • Her life continues to inspire patriots and women leaders.

Memorials & Legacy

  • Jhansi Fort and museums dedicated to her memory.
  • Numerous statues and memorials across India.
  • Remembered as the “Joan of Arc of India”.
  • Her valor celebrated in books, films, and folklore.
