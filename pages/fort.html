Red Fort (Lal Qila)

Book Tickets Plan Your Visit

Overview

Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan and completed in 1648, the Red Fort in Delhi served as the principal residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly two centuries. Its colossal ramparts, intricate pavilions, and ceremonial spaces embody the peak of Mughal architectural expression.

Key Highlights

  • Massive red sandstone walls stretching ~2.5 km.
  • Iconic spaces: Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Rang Mahal.
  • Annual Independence Day address by the Prime Minister.
  • Blend of Persian, Timurid, and Hindu architectural elements.
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Architecture & Design

Symmetry & Axial Planning

Grand gateways and linear courts align ceremonial and private zones with disciplined symmetry.

Marble & Inlay Work

Delicate pietra dura and carved marble screens soften the fort’s powerful sandstone geometry.

Gardens & Water

Charbagh gardens and water channels cool spaces and enhance movement, light, and reflection.

Red Fort

Historic Monument of Delhi | UNESCO World Heritage Site

Welcome to the Majestic Red Fort

Experience the grandeur of Mughal architecture and history in Delhi's iconic Lal Qila.

Key Facts

  • Location: Old Delhi, India
  • Constructed: 1639–1648 AD
  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori
  • Material: Red sandstone
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2007

Historical Significance

  • Palace fort of Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.
  • Seat of Mughal power for nearly 200 years.
  • Site of India’s first flag hoisting by PM Nehru on 15 August 1947.
  • Symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

Architecture

  • Walls: 2.5 km long, 18 meters high.
  • Complex includes Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Rang Mahal, gardens.
  • Style: Persian, Timurid, Indian.
  • Famous gates: Lahore Gate, Delhi Gate.

Visitor Information

  • Timings: 9:30 AM – 4:30 PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Tickets: ₹35 (Indians), ₹500 (Foreigners)
  • Photography allowed
  • Nearby attractions: Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk
