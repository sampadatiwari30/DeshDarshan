Dadabhai Naoroji

Father of Indian Nationalism | Economic Reformer

Welcome to the Legacy of Dadabhai Naoroji

Pioneer of Indian nationalism and the first Indian to become a British MP.

Key Facts

  • Full Name: Dadabhai Naoroji
  • Born: 4 September 1825, Bombay, India
  • Known as: Grand Old Man of India
  • Philosophy: Economic reform, political representation, education
  • Occupation: Educator, Politician, Economist

Historical Significance

  • First Indian to be elected to the British House of Commons (1892).
  • Exposed the Drain of Wealth theory in British India.
  • One of the founding members of the Indian National Congress.
  • Worked to bring Indian issues to the international stage.

Philosophy

  • Believed in economic reforms and fair treatment under British rule.
  • Advocated education, political representation, and social reform.
  • Promoted Indian self-governance through political awareness.
  • Inspired later leaders like Gandhi and Nehru.

Memorials & Legacy

  • Statues and memorials across India, including Mumbai and Delhi.
  • Recognized as a pioneering voice for Indian independence.
  • Educational institutions and awards named in his honor.
  • His writings still guide economic and political thought in India.
